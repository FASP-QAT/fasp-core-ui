The supply planning table on this screen uses the same formulas as in the Supply Plan screen. For guidance on how QAT calculates opening balance, suggested shipments, projected inventory, average monthly consumption, months of stock, and unmet demand, please refer to the Show Guidance on the Supply Planning screen.

  • Scenario: Increase forecasted consumption

    User-entered percentage over selected period. Only applies to months with forecasted consumption values and no actual consumption.

    Example:
    Increase forecast by 10% from January to March
    Forecast consumption: Jan = 100, Feb = 110, Mar = 112
    Increased forecast consumption: Jan = 100 x 1.1, Feb = 110 x 1.1, Mar = 112 x 1.1
    Increased forecast consumption: Jan = 110, Feb = 121, Mar = 123

  • Scenario: Decrease forecasted consumption

    User-entered percentage over selected period. Only applies to months with forecasted consumption values and no actual consumption.

    Example:
    Decrease forecast by 10% from January to March
    Forecast consumption: Jan = 100, Feb = 110, Mar = 112
    Decreased forecast consumption: Jan = 100 x 0.9, Feb = 110 x 0.9, Mar = 112 x 0.9
    Decreased forecast consumption: Jan = 90, Feb = 99, Mar = 101

  • Scenario: Remove unfunded shipments

    QAT removes all shipments in a user-selected period with a funding source = TBD, and therefore in Planned status.

  • Scenario: Remove Planned shipments

    QAT removes all shipments with status = Planned.
    User has the option to only remove Planned shipments that do not comply with the lead time based on the program’s lead time logic.

    Example:

    Removing all planned shipments
    Current month = Jan
    Lead time = 8 months
    Planned shipments for Feb, Apr, Jul, Oct, and Nov.
    New supply plan removes all five shipments.

    Removing all planned shipments not complying with the lead time
    Planned shipments for Feb, Apr, Jul, Oct, and Nov.
    New supply plan removes shipments from months between Jan and Sep (i.e, Jan + 8 months).
    New supply has planned shipments in Oct and Nov.

  • Scenario: Remove all Submitted shipments that do not comply with the lead time

    QAT removes all shipments with status = Submitted that do not comply with the lead time based on the program’s lead time logic.

    Example:

    Removing all submitted shipments that do not comply with the lead time
    Current month = Jan
    Lead time from Submitted to Received = 4 months
    Submitted shipments with received dates in Feb, Apr, Jul, Oct, and Nov.
    New supply plan removes Submitted shipments from months between Jan and May (i.e, Jan + 4 months).
    New supply has planned shipments in Jul, Oct, and Nov.

  • Scenario: Remove all On-hold shipments that do not comply with the lead time

    QAT removes all shipments with status = On-hold that do not comply with the lead time based on the program’s lead time logic.

  • Scenario: Re-plan supply plan

    For a user-entered period, QAT removes all Planned shipments from user-selected procurement agent(s) and funding source(s); then uses the suggested shipments logic to create new shipments that comply with lead time and MIN/MAX requirements

    Suggested Shipments Logic - “Plan by MOS”

    Note: Below is the logic for planning units that are planned by months of stock (MOS). Update the “plan by” setting in Update planning unit screen.

    Where Month N is the month QAT is calculating for:

    1. If AMC = 0 or N/A for Month N, no suggested shipment
    2. If Month N is stocked out (Ending Balance = 0), QAT will always suggest a shipment
      1. If both of next 2 months (N+1, N+2) are < Min MOS, suggested shipment will bring month N to Max MOS. Suggested qty = (Max MOS for N) * (AMC for N) – (Ending Balance for N) + (Unmet Demand for N)
      2. If 1 or both of the next 2 months (N+1 or N+2) is > Min MOS, suggested shipment will bring month N to Min MOS. Suggested qty = (Min MOS for N) * (AMC for N) – (Ending Balance for N) + (Unmet Demand for N)
    3. Is product is understocked (MOS < Min MOS) for 3 straight months (Month N, N+1, N+2)?
      1. If yes, suggested shipment to bring Month N to Max MOS. Suggested qty = (Max MOS for N) * (AMC for N) – (Ending Balance for N)
      2. If no, no suggested shipment
    Suggested Shipments - “Plan by Quantity”

    Note: Below is the logic for planning units that are planned by months of stock (MOS). Update the “plan by” setting in Update planning unit screen.

    Where Month N is the month QAT is calculating for, and X = Distribution Lead Time:

    1. If AMC = 0 or N/A for Month N, no suggested shipment
    2. If Month N is stocked out (Ending Balance = 0), QAT will always suggest a shipment
      1. If both of next 2 months (N+1, N+2) are < Min Qty, suggested shipment is the quantity that would bring Month N to Max Qty. Suggested Qty = (Max Qty for N) – (Ending Balance for N) + (Unmet Demand for N)
      2. If one or both of the next 2 months (N+1 or N+2) is > Min Qty, suggested shipment is the quantity that would bring Month N to Min Qty. Suggested Qty = (Min Qty) – (Ending Balance for N) + (Unmet Demand for N)
    3. Is product understocked (Ending Balance < Min Qty) for 3 straight months (Month N, N+1, N+2)?
      1. If yes, suggested shipment is the quantity that would bring Month N to Max Qty. Suggested Qty = (Max Qty for N) – (Ending Balance for N)
      2. If no, no suggested shipment

    QAT puts the suggested quantity in Month N–X. In other words, X months before Month N, where X is the Distribution Lead Time.
